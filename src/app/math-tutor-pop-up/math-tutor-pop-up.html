Peer Assist Learning Leader - Computer Math Fundamentals

Introduction

One of the main reasons I chose programming and earning a software engineering degree was the problem solving and mathematics. Often times I helped my professor Mrs. Samar Haddad for management, problem solving and providing different ways to make my classmates understand the complicated topics of Computer Math Fundamentals. Observing my passion and intrest, she provided me with position of Peer Assisted Learning Leader for Computer Math Fundamentals. My responsibilities involved taking over 3 - 1 hour lectures a week where I helped the juniors of 3 sections (91 students) to learn and understand the historically complicated concepts of mathematics.

Responsibilities

‣ In-charge of three weekly math tutorials for three sections (total 91 students) while assisting peers with demanding topics with subject expertise, critical thinking and problem-solving abilities making them understand the logic of various Computer Science concepts and its relationship with mathematics. ‣ Participated in biweekly meetings with the team and manager to expand and discuss the ways of assisting as well as promoting the sessions. Helped peers by clarifying and helping them practicing the concepts of CS in terms of the time and space complexity with an insight of tackling the problems of Hexadecimals, binary, Trees, etc. ‣ 72 students participated in the final reviews rating the sessions with an average of 4.3/5, with 1 being not helpful and 5 being very helpful.
